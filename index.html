Austin Haedicke, MA, LAPC

I am a Licensed Associate Professional Counselor (#APC005571) who works with children and andolescents (ages 5 - 18) under direction and supervision at Transitional Family Services.

I work primarily from a person / child centered perspective and employ play and narrative therapeutic techniques, however, I am adamant about adapting to the needs of my clients.

Services

I provide professional counseling services under supervision as part of a directed experience required to train Licensed Professional Counselors. This means that I utilize counseling and psychotherapy to evaluate and treat emotional and mental problems and conditions, whether cognitive, behavioral, or affective; suh as:

  • + Trauma, Sexual Abuse, Physical Abuse, Neglect,
  • + Anger, Anxiety, Depression, Fear, Emotional Instability,
  • + Attachment and Separation, Divorce, Death, and Grief

I believe that this is an in-depth, interpersonal, and collaborative process. I utlize an array of theoretical orientations including play, narrative, psychodynamic, and person-centered.

About Me

I have worked with children and adolescents in a variety of settings including inpatient and partial hospitalization as well as outpatient environments. I believe that play is the language of children and so it is the primary medium for assisting them in working through their difficulties.

Since 2014 I have been an active member of the American Academy of Psychotherapists. In 2015 I received my Master of Arts degree in Psychology from the University of West Georgia where I was also awarded the Mike Arons Scholarship for involvement in Humanistic Psychology.

Currently I am pursuing licensure as a professional counselor in the State of Georiga, as well as certification as a play therapist through The Association for Play Therapy.
